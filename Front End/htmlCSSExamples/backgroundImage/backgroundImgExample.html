As you can see, the two types of background sizing produces different results.

Contain - Makes the image fit the correct width and fit the whole image in, though it may not cover perfectly and may have to mess with aspect ratios.

Cover - Makes an image cover the entire container, though the image may cut off to make this happen.

Additionally the paralax square shows how backgrounds can be fixed in order to have it stick during scrolling. This requires a high quality image to scroll naturally.

There is also an additional container with two images, one underneath the other. The last shows how one can overlap another.

In order for overlapping to work as intended, the layer closest to the screen must be declared first.
